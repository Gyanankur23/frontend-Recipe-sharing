1. Chicken Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp ghee
  • 2 tbsp oil

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, turmeric, ginger garlic paste, and biryani masala for at least an hour.
  2. Cook the basmati rice until 70% done and set aside.
  3. Heat oil and ghee in a large pot, fry the onions until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy.
  5. Add marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is tender.
  6. Layer the rice over the chicken mixture, sprinkle chopped mint and coriander leaves.
  7. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  8. Serve hot with raita.

2. Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add tomato ketchup and garam masala. Mix well.
  5. Add cubed paneer and fresh cream. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.

3. Masoor Dal

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup masoor dal (red lentils)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak the masoor dal for 30 minutes.
  2. Pressure cook the dal with turmeric powder and salt until soft.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy.
  7. Add the cooked dal and red chili powder. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

4. Aloo Gobi

Ingredients:

  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy.
  5. Add turmeric, coriander, and red chili powder. Mix well.
  6. Add potatoes and cauliflower florets. Stir to coat with the spices.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with chapati or rice.

5. Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Oil for frying
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add milk to form a soft dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls, ensuring there are no cracks.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and fry the balls on low heat until golden brown.
  4. In a separate pot, combine sugar, water, and cardamom powder. Boil to make syrup.
  5. Soak the fried balls in the hot syrup for 2-3 hours.
  6. Garnish with saffron strands and chopped pistachios before serving.
